Sources & methodology

How our stories are made.

Our process balances compelling storytelling with evidence, transparency, and human review.

1

Topic selection

We select topics with a clear historical question, strong documentary potential, and enough credible material for verification.

2

Source review

We prioritize archives, museums, universities, official reports, historical institutions, reputable encyclopedias, and established journalism.

3

Script drafting

Technology may assist with structure and phrasing, but claims must remain consistent with available evidence.

4

Claim verification

Important claims are categorized as supported, disputed, partially supported, unsupported, or contradicted. Unsupported core claims are removed.

5

Production

Narration, imagery, captions, music, and editing are assembled into a vertical short-form video.

6

Final human review

Before publishing, we review the hook, factual framing, visuals, captions, audio, and platform metadata.

Historical uncertainty

Some events remain disputed. When credible sources disagree, we use cautious wording and identify uncertainty rather than presenting one interpretation as settled fact.
